Chapter 23


That was the last time the Feds ever got a clear look at me. From what I gather, Mordecai was able to extricate a large portion of the remaining funds. But he was never good at keeping a low profile

One morning I woke up and Mordecai’s face was splashed across CNN.com. The media had got ahold of grainy surveillance footage–it showed a seemingly-intoxicated Leroy Brown, leaving a bar in Malibu. According to eyewitnesses, Mordecai had made quite the scene at the bar in Malibu. Buying the entire bar round after round of shots, claiming he was one of the “Harvard Bandits.” Sources say he seemed extremely intoxicated.

Footage showed Mordecai leaving the premises at approximate 2:34 a.m. in a red Ferrari Spyder convertible. Police reports indicated the driver lost control of the Spyder about a mile down the road. The vehicle flipped a guardrail and tumbled into the Pacific. Crash scene photos on TMZ showed a grisly scene. The driver’s Ferrari crumpled like a wad of tin foil.

Police reports indicated that the body has not yet been recovered. They expected it to wash ashore within the next few days.


After things settled down, I laid low in the slums of Mexico City for two years. I cultivated the most epic tan, studied up on Spanish, and tried to blend in with the locals. Sure I got a few stares, but I was living in abject poverty. My room cost just 3,000 pesos, or about $235 USD per month. I stayed current on the storage rental fees–where the loot was stashed, back in the States–and kept my nose clean and head down.
