Chapter 23


That was the last time the Feds ever got a clear look at me. From what I gather, Mordecai was able to extricate a large portion of the remaining funds. But he was never good at keeping a low profile

One morning I woke up to Mordecai’s face, splashed across CNN.com. The media had got ahold of grainy surveillance footage–it showed a seemingly-intoxicated Lawrence “Leroy” Brown, leaving a bar in Malibu. According to eyewitnesses, Brown had made quite the scene at the bar. Footage showed Mordecai leaving the premises at approximately 2:34 a.m. in a red Ferrari Spyder convertible. Police reports indicate the driver lost control of the Spyder shortly thereafter. The vehicle flipped a guardrail and tumbled into the Pacific. Crash scene photos on TMZ showed a grisly scene. The driver’s Ferrari had crumpled like a wad of tin foil.

The article indicated that a body had not yet been recovered. Police expected it to wash ashore within days.


After things settled down, I laid low in the slums of Mexico City for two years. I cultivated the most epic tan, honed my Spanish and tried to blend in with the locals. Sure I got a few stares, but I was living in abject poverty. My room cost just 2400 pesos, or about $175 USD per month.
