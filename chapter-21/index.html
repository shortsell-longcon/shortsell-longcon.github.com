Chapter 21


Nothing.


Just kidding. It was all there. The cash, and the precious metal–which turned out to be solid gold bars (bullion).

Like a banshee I loaded the cash and gold into the UHaul. It took a helluva long time. Probably forty-five minutes in all.

After getting a few blocks away, lo and behold, approaching me on the side street is a red corvette. I pulled my Yankees cap down even more, but I could see Mordecai checking me out as I passed. About fifteen feet behind me, Mordecai slams on the breaks and does that drifting thing (like in The Fast and the Furious). He slams on the gas as we begin … I would call it, a medium-speed chase.

Shit, I’m not going to outrun this fucker. UHaul van vs. Corvette equals epic fail.

We approached downtown Fayetteville. I sped up and turned down an alley. I made a hard right and stopped on a dime, as soon as I was out of sight. A few seconds later, I threw the gear in reverse and hit the gas.

My van slammed into his corvette, crumpling its hood. In the mirror, I saw Mordecai’s face. It was bloody. What had I done? Sure he double-crossed me, but I could have killed him! And then, as if on cue, Mordecai bolted upright, threw off his seatbelt, and sprinted towards me. Sirens whaled in the distance.

I was in too deep; there was no turning back.


I made it to the freeway. Two exits down, I pulled off and down onto a long country road. I found a slightly wooded area that I could pull the van into. Trees obscured the view from the street. More sirens yelped in the distance.
