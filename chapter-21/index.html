Chapter 21


Nothing.


Just kidding. It was all there. The cash, the metal–which turned out to be solid gold bars–bullion.

Like a banshee I loaded the cash and gold into the UHaul. It took a helluva long time. Probably forty-five minutes in all.

When I got a few blocks away, I looked in horror as a red Corvette pulled onto the street. I played it cool in my Yankees cap and dark sunglasses, but I saw Mordecai checking me out as I passed. Ten feet behind me, Mordecai hit the breaks and drifted, turning his corvette around to chase me. I hit the gas.

Shit, I’m not going to outrun this fucker. UHaul vs. Corvette equals epic fail.

A high-speed chase ensued through the streets of Fayetteville. When I got downtown, I turned down an alley. At the end of the alley I turned right, just out of sight. Threw the gear in reverse and waited a few seconds before hitting the gas.

The truck slammed into the corvette, crumpling its roof. In the mirror I saw the bloodied face of Mordecai. And for an instant I felt bad about wounding my once best friend. In the distance, sirens.


I made it to the freeway. Two exits down, I pulled off and onto a long country road. I found a slightly wooded area where I could pull the truck behind and out of sight from the street. Sirens waled in the distance.
