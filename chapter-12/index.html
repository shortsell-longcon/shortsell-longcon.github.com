Chapter 12


The proverbial shit hit the fan one day when a gentleman security blogger, Spruce Breiner, posted about our hardware keystroke loggers being found in the offices of several major corporations. The company execs had tried to cover up the breach, but word leaked out after several security analysts shared a similar tale over beers at a conference.

It was being held in Las Vegas that year. At a table for twelve, three representatives from three of the companies we hit, pulled out replicas of the exact same keystroke loggers that we’d used.

Sure enough, they were all the same. Word got out to Spruce Breiner, who went public with the news.

With egg on their face, the IT departments of the companies we’d hit (who discovered our loggers) had no choice but to go to the feds.

The tables were turning.

Breiner’s blog is read by tens of thousands of security professionals daily. He’s the Big Swinging Dick, when it comes to IT security. I was on IRC when someone posted the link to Breiner’s blog post.

He laid it all out there. Of course, he guessed wrong on some of the points. He thought we were a Russian crime syndicate, infiltrating corporations in order to pilfer consumer credit card data. I giggled at that thought, picturing some schlub Russian hacker eeking out $15k per month on pilfered credit card data.

“To be on the safe side,” Breiner wrote, “check all hardware connections between keyboards, mice and other USB devices. If you see a small black piece of hardware in between any of your peripherals and their USB port, alert your IT staff immediately.”


It was time to ramp up our trades. Go for the next $100 mil before the federalies came knocking on our door.

It was simple, really. We consolidated our efforts in an attempt to find one giant, massive, epic swinging trade. The kind day-traders have wet dreams about. A real Black Swan event, in the flesh.

A one day swing that could net us $75 million, easy.

No small feat. Needles in haystacks, dugga dugga.

We redoubled our efforts and probed existing targets using Raptor’s ever-expanding search capabilities, thanks to the tireless work of Raj.


Meanwhile, Operation Charley was humming along steadily. I had Raj go to work on finding potentially libelous or criminal activity, buried within an archive of corporate documents. The moneyshot, if you will.

Within a week he’d written a prototype in python. Not my favorite language, but great for banging out quick hacks. I set it to work on the treasure trove of documents we’d pilfered from Doldrum Macks. The results came back after several minutes.

Jaw, meet floor.

I stared at the screen. It was all there.

I waded through several false positives before getting to the real dirt. Among the scandalous documents were the following:

a) A corporate memo to senior VPs, the CFO and CEO, from a junior accountant. It stated that the mortgage-backed securities the firm was trading in were 40-60% fraudulent. e.g. missing paperwork, forged signatures, invalid proof of income, etc, etc. The firm continued trading these securities for months, knowing full well they were mostly a giant house of cards.

b) Fraudulent accounting practices in their tax-services division.

c) Clear lack of separation between their analyst and ibanking divisions. This amounts to, essentially, insider trading on their part. Their M&A department knew of upcoming mergers. The ibanker on the deal would give a heads up to their ratings & analyst division, who would then upgrade both stocks to STRONG BUY. This makes their advisors and traders look smarter–but it’s only because they were using intel not available to the general public.

d) Dealings with a Big Chemical company who’s shoddy workmanship led to the deaths of several hundred workers in southern India. Moldmen Lax was brought in to broker the deal. They pushed hard, initially, to settle the victims’ claims without any acknowledgment of wrongdoing. The maximum payment allotted to victims’ families? $125 USD per worker. Sickening.


I encrypted the files in a zip archive and passed it along to Charley, Mordecai and Sal.

It would be our one piece of leverage when the shit finally hit the fan.
