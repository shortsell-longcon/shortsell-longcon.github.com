Chapter 24


After two years time, I felt the heat had died down enough. It was time to finally enjoy the fruits of my labor. My face was no longer being splashed across the front page of the Post or Times. Sure, Wench and his boys would love nothing more than to bring me in. But even Wench had moved on to other cases.

I wrote to him: “If I ever fail to contact you for any period lasting more than ninety days, you are instructed to carry out my final wishes. As your final payment, you will be receiving $1.5 million USD in unmarked bills, delivered via private courier.”


So by now you might be wondering why I decided to put down this tale–a tale so rife with self-incrimination … well, I’ll tell you why.

I wanted my side of the story told, especially when it became clear that I might not be around to tell it. I always thought I’d end up in Club Fed, but what if someone got to me before the federalies?

I’ve forwarded a copy of this manuscript to Sal. If you’re reading this, it means the Dead Man’s Switch has been triggered. I’ve instructed him to find a publisher, in the event I “go dark,” as they say.

So anyway, I was watching The Firm (the old movie, not the new TV show) in my condo, early this morning. It’s one of those high-rise condominium towers in Rio de Janeiro. That’s where I’ve been living since gathering the loot and making my arrangements. It was 3:17 a.m. when I heard a loud rap rap rap on the door. Sometimes I order in room service, but not last night.
