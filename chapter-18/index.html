Chapter 18


Armed with Mordecai’s list of aliases, Jimmy went to work on locating our man. He struck gold at the Hyatt Regency.

“Hello,” said Jimmy, “can you patch me through to Kensington Danforth? It’s urgent. He’s staying in room 742.”

“Sure, just a second.” A beat. “Sir, he’s staying in room 420. I’ll patch you through.”

Bingo.

Jimmy setup a surveillance operation at the Hyatt; meanwhile I posted up outside Arkansas National Bank.

After a day of that, I grew restless. A trip to a local spy shop yielded the necessary tools to simply my life. Posing as an electrician, I installed two spy cams across the street from the bank. One above a coffee shop, the other beneath a neon “Diner” sign. With a lens the size of a button, the cameras were nearly invisible to the naked eye. They beamed a live feed to a nearby 3G modem. From there the data was sent directly to my server farm in Amsterdam.

I sat back, turned on HBO (quite a perk for the cheap motel), watched, and waited.


That night, Leroy showed up at the hotel, wasted. He was with some chick who was sporting thigh-high, black leather boots.

Jimmy said, “She’s quite the looker.”

“Yeah, that’s one thing you should know. Leroy’s quite the fan of the ladies. Paid or not.”


The next day, I get a text from Jimmy: “Bishop has left the palace.”

A few hours later I’m watching the live feed, when Jimmy pulls up in a Lincoln Town Car. He’s dressed to the nines in a pinstripe suit. Two women, who appear to be some kind of assistants, step out of the vehicle. “Bishop is in the pocket,” I text Jimmy.

He’d lost the tail at the airport parking lot, so it was a good thing we had the feed.

From what emerged of the case later, we know that Leroy had opened the bank account under the name “ATM Distributors Global, Inc.” There was a major ATM distributor by the same name, only, without the Global. These are the people who made a fortune by stocking every mini-part, strip joint and fast-food shop with an ATM.

The women turned out to be high-end escorts, of course. Apparently the branch manager wanted to meet the man behind such a large cash infusion to the bank.

“Our business is very cash-intensive, as you can imagine,” Leroy told the manager. “We’ve blanketed the Northeast with our machines, but see a huge opportunity for us to expand in the south. Please, don’t mention this to the press, but we are about to acquire one of our major competitors in the Southeast. Hence the infusion of capital in this market.”

“Of course, Mr. Danforth. And we are glad to have your business. Thank you for meeting me in person. Is there anything else we can help you with today?”

Mordecai looked to his assistants. “The withdrawals,” one said.

“Ah yes,” said Mordecai, “we will begin making withdrawals next week. Does Tuesday work for you?”

“Of course. How much are we talking about?”

“Twenty-five million.”

The branch manager gulped, hard.

“That’s quite the sum.”

“Our southern operations are doing quite well of late. Businesses, wishing to avoid fees from the credit card companies, have been installing our new line of machines in record number.”

“I see.”

“We’ll need an armored car to transport the funds to our secure holding facility. Will there be a charge for that?”

“No, Mr. Danforth. Let me check with my boss, but I believe we’d be happy to provide that service to a client such as yourself.”

“Great. I believe that’s all. Shall we?”

“It’s a pleasure doing business with you,” said the branch manager.

“And you, too.”


While Mordecai was doing business at the bank, I broke into his hotel room. It was simple, really. Those new hotel room keys can be reverse-engineered quite easily. Using a magnetic card writer, I made myself a key to Mordecai’s room.

The lamp fixture provided some camouflage for a hidden mic. Off in the corner of the room, I installed a spy cam. By the time I ducked out to my rental car, the feed was live.


The next morning, after a night of carnal activities with his “assistants,” Mordecai made a call. The mic audio was kind of fuzzy. All I heard was: “Yeah, this is Kensington … ATM Distributors … I’ll be … an hour … 5150 Oa… I’ll see you there.”

Jimmy lost the tale on Mordecai’s red Corvette convertible. Mordecai was savvy enough to drive out to the airport and swap cars before meeting with associates.

Google Maps’ auto-complete feature narrowed down the address to two options: Oak Avenue and Oak Dale Drive. Jimmy took the Avenue while I scoped out the Drive.

“It’s here,” texted Jimmy. I made my way over.

Mordecai had really outdone himself. It was a large warehouse in an industrial district. “ATM Distributors Global, Inc.” read the freshly-painted sign.

I had a sinking feeling in my stomach. Leroy had really stepped up his game. I briefly, briefly considered turning states evidence and narcing out Mordecai. Or phoning in an anonymous tip to the Feds.

Just then three large trucks pulled up to the address. Jimmy and I watched from down the black. Mordecai directed traffic as the construction workers carried large steel sheets into the building.

The fucker. He was construction a vault.
