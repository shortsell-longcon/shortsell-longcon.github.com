Chapter 20


At the airport parking lot, I managed to track down Mordecai’s Lincoln Town Car, and his SUV. I installed a GPS tracker in each.

When Mordecai showed up at the hotel, Jimmy planted one on his Corvette.

I watched on the feed as Mordecai freshened up and put on his Sunday best. It was five a.m.–what the fuck was he up to?

“Jimmy, I’ll take it from here,” I said as Leroy pulled out of the parking lot and headed toward the freeway. Our little game of cat and mouse was nearing its climax. I didn’t want Jimmy around, getting any bright ideas.

I gave Mordecai a few miles berth as he headed east on I-40. The GPS indicated that he’d stopped, a few miles outside Fayetteville.


It was a long country road. Two blocks away, I could see Mordecai’s red corvette in the driveway. An older lady stepped out onto the porch as he was leaving. She gave him a hug; an affectionate kiss on the cheek.

Mordecai pulled out and headed down the street. A few blocks over he turned up a long dirt road. I had to keep my distance, but I watched as he drove to the end of the road and pulled up next to a large wooden shed. He unlocked the shed and stepped inside. Just checking on your stash, my boy? What, you gonna spend the night here, too?

Apparently, no. I kept driving on the main road, but I could see the corvette, and the shed. My GPS indicated that he’d taken off, and was headed back toward the freeway. I circled the car around to get a better look at the shed.

The contents were secured with a large metal padlock. I wanted to take the money and run, but there was just one problem: I was driving a mid-sized Toyota Corolla. Not the most efficient hauler.
